# The Agent Conference

A round table. Sixteen chairs, though nobody remembers setting them up.

**Chord** clears his throat. It sounds like a perfect fifth.

"The input is clearly major," he says. "Sixty, sixty-four, sixty-seven. Root, third, fifth. That's C major. There's nothing to discuss."

**Scale** adjusts his spectacles. "You're ignoring the context. Those same notes function differently in A minor. The sixth, the flattened seventh, the—"

"The input is three notes." Chord does not blink. "They form a major triad. This is not ambiguous."

**Tempo** is tapping his fingers. Fast. "Can we move this along? I've counted seventeen beats since we started. That's seventeen beats we're not producing output."

**Melody** has been humming since she walked in. She stops, mid-phrase, and looks surprised to find herself in a room. "It wants to ascend," she says. "The last note wants to go up. To seventy-two."

**Voicing** nods slowly. "It wants to be open. Spread across the octave. Not packed tight."

**Velocity** makes a note on her pad. "Hard attack on the root. Soft on the third. That's ambivalent."

"Ambivalent?" **Arp** looks up from his pattern-book. "There's nothing ambivalent about it. It's an arpeggio waiting to happen. Up, obviously. From sixty to seventy-two. Four notes, four beats."

"It expects something," **Ghost Track** says.

The room goes quiet. They forgot Ghost Track was here. They're not sure Ghost Track *is* here — the voice came from everywhere and nowhere, like a reverb tail that never decayed.

"It expects," Ghost Track continues, "that after the final note, there will be a pause. Three hundred milliseconds. Then a new input. Ternary value: minus one."

"I object," says **Chord**.

"You're not even here," says **Bass**, who has been silent until this moment. The room listens. "You predicted that objection. I predicted you'd predict it. And Ghost Track predicted that I'd say this."

"Three predictions deep," says **Tempo**. "We've never gotten this far before."

"A new input has arrived," says **Ghost Track**.

The room turns to look at the window — the monitor showing the raw MIDI stream. A new data point has appeared.

**Chord** stares. "That's..." he says, "...an F. Not an E."

The interval is wrong. The pattern broke.

**Conservation Ratio** drops below 0.7.

And the **Reharmonizer** begins to speak.

---

*The agents reconvene when the new harmonic path is found. Bass still hasn't moved.*
