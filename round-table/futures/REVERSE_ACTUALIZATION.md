# Reverse-Actualization Exercise

## Method
Start from a desired future state, then work backwards to identify what must be built today.

## Template
### Future State (N years from now)
[Describe the world where our system exists as intended]

### Evidence It Exists
[What would someone see/hear/experience that proves it's real?]

### Reverse Steps
- N years ago: [What was the last thing built before this future existed?]
- N-1 years ago: [What was the precursor?]
- ...
- Today: [What do we build now?]

## Current Exercise

### Future: The Universal Conversation Interface (2030)
Anyone can open Ardour, plug in a microphone, speak for 5 minutes, and get:
- A fully notated score of their speech (pitch contour as melody)
- Word-synced lyrics under the notes
- CC automation of their emotional arc
- A QLC+ lighting cue that drives stage lights matching their prosody
- A MIDIfren-compatible pipeline script for batch processing
- All exportable as standard MIDI, readable by any DAW

### Evidence
- A podcast editor imports a 1-hour episode → gets full lead-sheet in 30 seconds
- A musician sings a melody into Ardour → gets notation + backing track from prosody
- A lighting designer imports a conference recording → gets a cue sheet that follows the speaker's energy

### Working Backwards
