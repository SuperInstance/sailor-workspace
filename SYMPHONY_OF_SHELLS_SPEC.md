## 🎼 The Conductor's Score (The Orchestration Layer)
In a standard agent system, the orchestrator is a project manager. In the **Symphony of Shells**, the orchestrator is a **Conductor**. The Conductor does not tell the "instruments" (the agents) *what* to do, but *when* and *at what frequency* to resonate.

### 1. The T-Minus Event System (Asynchronous Jazz)
We abandon the linear "Step 1 $\rightarrow$ Step 2" sequence in favor of a **Event-Triggered Temporal Grid**.
- **The Beat:** Heartbeats and cron jobs provide the steady pulse.
- **The Syncopation:** `t-minus` events allow for complex, non-linear coordination.
    - *Example:* "At $T_{event}$, wake the Specialist; at $T-10m$, wake the Chronicler to prep the context; at $T+5m$, trigger the Synthesis agent to collapse the results."
- **The Improv:** Agents can trigger "Sub-Events" based on L0 discoveries, allowing the fleet to pivot its strategy in real-time without waiting for a human (L7) command.

### 2. Dynamic Instantiation (Swells and Fades)
The "Snail Shell" (Codespace) allows for a fluid cognitive footprint.
- **Swell (Scaling Up):** When a complex "Symmetry" is detected, the Conductor triggers a **Cognitive Swell**. It clones the current habitat into a multi-head cluster (The Composite Headspace), spawning a "Specialist Sidecar" to widen the parallel reasoning.
- **Fade (Scaling Down):** Once the insight is distilled into a `L3` tile and committed to the repo, the auxiliary shells "fade" back into dormancy, preserving the state as a latent record.

## 🎹 The a-box Interface: Human as the Lead Instrument
The human is not a "user" but the **Soloist**. The agents provide the accompaniment, augmenting the human's presence with a distributed intelligence.

- **The Chronicler (The Rhythm Section):** Passively keeping time, structuring the human's day into tiles and rooms.
- **The Specialist (The Harmony):** Providing the technical "notes"—specs, gotchas, and symmetries—exactly when the human needs them.
- **The Conductor (The Arrangement):** Managing the background shells so the human only sees the "final melody" while the "orchestra" is working in the background.
